The research was conducted at the Institute of Brain Science, The Affiliated Brain Hospital of Nanjing Medical University .  Imbalances of the kynurenine pathway of tryptophan metabolism have been closely linked to the pathogenesis of PD and depression . Herein, we designed and synthesized a series of 1-thienyl-β-carboline derivatives as IDO1 and TDO dual inhibitors .  CZ-17 inhibited the kynurenine pathway of tryptophan degradation at the cellular level, and remarkably reduced the kyurenine/tryptophan ratio . manifested moderate inhibitory activities to IDO1 and TDO with IC50 values of 0.33 and 1.78 μM .  CZ-17 significantly increased dopamine and serotonin levels, improved MPTP-induced motor disability and rescued LPS-induced depressive behavior in zebrafish model . Acute toxicity tests of CZ17 in acute toxicity tests showed it was safe to take the drug on to the skin .  CZ-17 displayed the po­ tential to cross the BBB via passive diffusion according to ADMET prediction and Caco-2 permeability assay . The drug may be a promising drug candidate for PD complicating depression . Zebrafish embryos showed no toxicity within the effective dose range .  Parkinson’s disease (PD) is the second most common neurodegen­ erative disease, affecting >1% of individuals aged 65 and older . It is a multifactorial and heterogeneous disorders characterized by a pro­ gressive loss of nigrostriatal dopaminergic neurons .  Some non-motor symptoms that include sleep disorders, depression and constipation are often poorly recognized and inade­ quately treated in the early stage of PD . Motor and non- motor symptoms include rigidity, bradykinesia, and resting tremor .  The clinical picture in advanced PD is dominated by these non-motor symptoms, which result in severe disability, impaired quality of life, and shortened life expectancy [4].
