Both depression and cancer are related to dysregulation of inflammatory and immune pathways . Depression is associated with increased expression of interferon-γ, interleukin-1β, and tumor necrosis factor α (TNF-α) In contrast, reductions of the activity of major histocompatibility are seen in depression .  Cancer patients present elevated levels of TNF-α, reduced major histocompatibility complex class I and II, and natural killer cells . Indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase (IDO), the rate-limiting enzyme of the enzyme of an enzyme, is also observed .  Tryptophan catabolite (TRYCAT) pathway, is induced by interferon-γ, interleukin-6, TNF-α, and oxidative stress . IDO catabolizes tryptophans, the amino acid precursor of serotonin and melatonin . TRYCAT pathway activation is accompanied by downregulation of TRYCATs .  The increase in IDO activity in tumor microenvironments is related to tumor cell escape from immune surveillance . Despite the evidence of inflammatory mechanisms underlying cancer and depression, it is important to emphasize that both diseases are linked to inflammation . of immune cell proliferation, function, and survival .  TRYCATs – synthesis of which increases in depression and cancer – are a link between the two diseases, and whether IDO is IDO . The purpose of this review is to examine whether detrimental TRYATs are a pathophysiological link between depression and cancers .  a potential pharmacological target for the treatment of the comorbid depression and cancer.
